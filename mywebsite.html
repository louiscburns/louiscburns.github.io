About Me

I am a first-year graduate student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln in the department of mathematics; my interests are in algebra, geometry, analysis, and combinatorics. As a senior at Pomona College, I worked with Professor Max Hlavacek on the Ehrhart Theory of poset polytopes. In particular, we were interested in constructing polytopes out of signed polytopes.

In the summer of 2023, I worked with Professor Alex Barrios (University of St. Thomas) at Pomona Research in Mathematics Experience (PRiME) working on classifying minimal discriminants for a family of elliptic curves. You may find slides for presentations below.

Outside of mathematics, I like to play pickleball, read, hike, and birdwatch.
